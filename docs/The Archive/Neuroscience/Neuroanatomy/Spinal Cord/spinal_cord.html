Pathway

The spinal cord exits the foramen magnum and extends to approximately the L1 vertebral level1.

Composition

The spinal cord is made up of both white and gray matter.

White matter

The majority of the white matter consists of the ascending (sensory) tracts and descending (motor) tracts1.

The rest of the white matter is composed of myelinated axons of interneurons.

Gray matter

Cross- Section

  • Dorsal Section: Contains neurons involved in sensory function1
  • Middle portion: contains interneurons1
  • Ventral section: contains neurons involved in motor function (anterior horn cells) that project to the peripheral muscles1

Spinal Nerve Roots

  • 31 pairs of spinal nerves:

Cervical

  • 8 pairs
  • Exit horizontally (relatively)1
  • C1-C7 Exit above the corresponding vertebrae1
  • C8 exits below the C7 vertebrae1

Thoracic

  • 12 pairs
  • Exit downwardly1
  • Do not correspond to vertebral level1

Lumbar

  • 5 lumbar
  • Exit downwardly1
  • Do not correspond to vertebral level1

Sacral

  • 5 sacral
  • Exit downwardly1
  • Do not correspond to vertebral level1

Coccygeal

  • 1 coccygeal
  • Exit downwardly1
  • Do not correspond to vertebral level1
