Plantar reflex

The tibial n. serves as the afferent aspect in the plantar reflex loop.

Plantar reflex is a superficial reflex where stroking of the lateral sole of the calcaneus to the base of 5th metatarsal results in toe plantarflexion.

An abnormal plantar reflex refers to when you stroke the foot and the 1st toe dorsiflexes while toes 2-5 abduct. This abnormal plantar reflex is known as a positive âBabinski signâ and is often observed in patients with corticospinal/UMN lesions.

Dysfunction

Entrapment sites:

  • Indirect
  • Direct
    • Soleus origin
    • Tarsal tunnel

Palpation

  • Patient positioned in prone
  • Palpate lateral head of the gastrocnemius at the distal joint line of the tibiofemoral joint.
  • Move medially and you should be able to strum the tibial nerve as it runs vertically.
  • Follow the tibial nerve inferiorly to palpate where it passes through the soleus origin.
    • This can be a place where the tibial nerve can be entrapped1.

Treatment

  • Soleus origin release
