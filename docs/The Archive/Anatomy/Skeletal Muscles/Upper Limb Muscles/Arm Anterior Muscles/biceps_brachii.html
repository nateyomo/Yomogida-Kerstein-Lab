Long Head

Origin

  • Supraglenoid tubercle of scapula2,3
  • Adjacent rim of Glenoid Labrum3

Insertion

  • Radial tuberosity2
  • Bicipital aponeurosis2

Innervation

Musculocutaneous N. (C5, C6)2,3

Action

  • Shoulder: Flexion
  • GHJ: Abduction, Internal rotation, Stabilization of humeral head during deltoid contraction2
  • Elbow: Flexion; Supination2
Note

The biceps brachii long head is also involved in abduction and a rupture will result in ~20% decrease in abduction strength5.

Short Head

Origin

  • Coracoid process of Scapula2

Insertion

  • Radial tuberosity2
  • Bicipital aponeurosis2

Innervation

Musculocutaneous N. (C5, C6)2

Action

  • Shoulder: flexion2
  • GHJ: Abduction, Internal rotation, Stabilization of humeral head during deltoid contraction2
  • Elbow: Flexion, Supination2
Note

Note: When the elbow is flexed, the biceps brachii acts as a powerful supinator because the lever arm is almost perpendicular to the axis of pronation/supination.2

Anatomy

  • Biceps Brachii Long Head tendon moves laterall from its origin, makes a right angle to place it in the anterior aspect of the humerus6
